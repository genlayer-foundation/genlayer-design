About GenLayer

A world where trust is autonomous and universal

GenLayer is the adjudication layer for the agentic economy — the missing piece of infrastructure that lets contracts resolve questions requiring judgment, not just code.

For all their promise, code-only blockchains share a blind spot: they can execute deterministic logic, but they cannot interpret. They cannot weigh context, read intent, or reach a verdict on anything that lives outside a strict if-then. As autonomous agents begin to transact, negotiate, and contract with one another, that gap becomes the bottleneck of the entire agentic economy.

GenLayer closes it. We are building a decentralized digital jurisdiction where every decision is validated by a diverse network of AI arbiters, reaching consensus the way a panel of judges might — independently, transparently, and on the record.

Intelligent Contracts become self-interpreting, self-executing, and self-enforcing.

Intelligent Contracts

The core primitive is the Intelligent Contract: an agreement written in natural language and code together. It can read a web page, evaluate a claim, or judge whether a condition has truly been met — then act on that judgment without a middleman. The contract understands what it is being asked, validates the outcome by consensus, and records a result that is final and challengeable in the open.

Optimistic Democracy

Verdicts are reached through Optimistic Democracy. A contract poses a question; AI validators reason about it independently; a majority verdict emerges and finalizes on-chain after an optimistic window during which anyone can challenge it. The result is a consensus mechanism that scales human-like judgment without surrendering the guarantees that make a blockchain worth trusting.
